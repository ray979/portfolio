About Me

Ever since I was little, I always had an obsession with learning how things function and finding solutions to problems. I would take apart old toys and electronics trying to figure out how each part worked. And when there is something to fix in the house whether it be the washing machine or the car, I'll be there to tinker with the problem.

That is why I chose to pursue computer engineering at the University of California, Irvine. A major which overlaps code and hardware. With programming and building circuits, I get to use my problem-solving skills to program/create something functional and useful. It is also fun to see how every circuit component or line of code contributes together to create a complete system. In a complete circuit, you could have many resistors, inductors, capacitors, and transistors (bipolar, MOSFETS, …etc). In a big program or application, you could have a bunch of classes, functions, and variables. And finally, in a computer system, you need the interaction of both code and hardware.

Outside programming and circuits, I enjoy gardening, fishing, and watching tv shows.

Projects

ANT-Automated Nutrient Technique

Senior Design Project. The objective of this project is to create an automated hydroponic system utilizing the hydroponic technique, Nutrient Film Technique. At the core of ANT is a Raspberry PI that monitors the hydroponic system through multiple sensors. The system will automatically dose the water in the reservoir with fertilizer or PH solution to keep the plants fed and the water PH balanced.

TIRSLK Max Quick Turn Wall Sprint

Wall sprint final for Spring 2022 EECS 195: Embedded Systems at UCI. Won first place in the final for the fastest wall sprint. To pull this off, I used the tachometer to keep track of how much the robot spun during the 180-degree turnaround. This allowed the robot to turn at a really fast speed or any speed without over-turning or under-turning. Also used the reflectance sensors and a simplified PID control of the wheel motors to make the sure the robot stays within the track boundary.
